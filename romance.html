It Ends With Us

By Colleen Hoover

It Ends With Us by Colleen Hoover follows Lily Bloom, a young woman who falls in love with Ryle Kincaid, a successful neurosurgeon. As their relationship grows, Ryle’s controlling and abusive tendencies emerge, forcing Lily to confront painful truths about love and self-worth. When her first love, Atlas Corrigan, returns, Lily must make difficult choices to break the cycle of abuse and find her own path.

4.17

The novel is widely praised for its raw, emotional storytelling and realistic portrayal of complex relationships. Readers appreciate Lily’s depth as a character and the sensitive handling of difficult themes like domestic abuse. While some find certain plot points predictable, the book is considered powerful, thought-provoking, and moving, earning an approximate rating of 4.3–4.5/5.

The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo

By Taylor Jenkins Reid

The story of legendary Hollywood actress Evelyn Hugo, who chooses to give a rare interview to young journalist Monique Grant. As Evelyn recounts her glamorous and scandal-filled life, she reveals the truth behind her seven marriages, her rise to stardom, and the sacrifices she made for love, fame, and happiness. The novel explores themes of ambition, identity, love, and the cost of living a life in the public eye.

4.30

The novel is widely acclaimed for its compelling storytelling, rich character development, and exploration of love, fame, and personal sacrifice. Readers praise Evelyn’s complex, multi-dimensional personality and the novel’s emotional depth, with its mix of glamour, heartbreak, and secrets keeping readers engaged. It is considered both captivating and thought-provoking, earning an approximate rating of 4.3–4.6/5.

Love and Other Words

By Christina Lauren

It follows Macy Sorensen and Elliot Petropoulos, childhood sweethearts whose relationship is interrupted by life circumstances. Years later, they reconnect as adults, confronting past regrets, lost love, and the chance to rekindle their romance. The novel explores themes of first love, second chances, and the enduring impact of memories on the heart.

4.29

The novel is praised for its heartfelt and emotional storytelling, with a nostalgic portrayal of first love and second chances. Readers appreciate the depth of the characters and the way past and present intertwine, making the romance feel genuine and moving. It is considered touching and beautifully written, earning an approximate rating of 4.2–4.5/5.

2 States

By Chetan Bhagat

It is a contemporary novel that follows Krish Malhotra and Ananya Swaminathan, a couple from different Indian states with contrasting cultural backgrounds. As they fall in love during college, they face the challenge of convincing their traditional families to accept their inter-state relationship. The novel humorously explores love, cultural differences, and the complexities of family approval in India.

4.27

The novel is praised for its lighthearted, humorous take on love and cultural differences in India. Readers enjoy the relatable portrayal of young romance and the challenges of family expectations. While some critique its simplicity and writing style, it is widely considered entertaining and engaging, earning an approximate rating of 3.8–4.1/5.

A Touch of Eternity

By Durjoy Datta

It tells the story of Druvan and Anvesha, soulmates born at the same moment, whose love faces opposition from their families. Determined to be together, they join a groundbreaking experiment that explores the boundaries of life, death, and reincarnation. As they navigate scientific challenges and emotional obstacles, the novel examines whether true love can endure against all odds, blending romance, suspense, and philosophical reflections on destiny and the human soul.

4.28

The novel is praised for its imaginative blend of romance, suspense, and philosophical themes. Readers enjoy the intense emotional connection between Druvan and Anvesha and the unique exploration of love, life, and reincarnation. While some note that the scientific aspects are lightly explored, the story is engaging and thought-provoking, earning an approximate rating of 4.0–4.3/5.
