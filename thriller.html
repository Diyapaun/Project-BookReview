The Silent Patient

By Alex Michaelides

Alicia Berenson, a famous painter, shoots her husband and stops speaking, refusing to utter a word. Criminal psychotherapist Theo Faber becomes obsessed with uncovering her silence, delving into her past to reveal a shocking truth that no one sees coming.

4.17

“The Silent Patient is a gripping psychological thriller that keeps you on edge from start to finish. Alex Michaelides masterfully explores trauma, obsession, and secrets, creating a tense and immersive story. The combination of Alicia’s silent perspective and Theo’s investigation builds suspense brilliantly, leading to a jaw-dropping twist that leaves readers stunned. A must-read for fans of mysteries and psychological thrillers.”

Verity

By Colleen Hoover

Struggling writer Lowen Ashleigh is hired to finish bestselling author Verity Crawford’s books and uncovers a hidden manuscript full of dark secrets. As she grows close to Verity’s husband, Jeremy, she is drawn into a tense web of suspense, lies, and shocking truths.

4.30

Verity is a gripping psychological thriller that blends suspense, romance, and dark secrets. Colleen Hoover builds tension masterfully, keeping readers on edge as Lowen uncovers Verity’s hidden manuscript and navigates moral dilemmas. The ending is shocking and unpredictable, leaving a lingering sense of unease and making the story impossible to forget.

A Good Girl's Guide To Murder

By Holly Jackson

High schooler Pip Fitz-Amobi reopens a five-year-old murder case for her final-year project, investigating the death of Andie Bell and the alleged killer, Sal Singh. As Pip digs deeper, she uncovers hidden secrets, lies, and shocking truths in her seemingly quiet town, putting herself in danger while piecing together the mystery.

4.29

Holly Jackson delivers a smart, fast-paced thriller with a mix of suspense, clever twists, and well-developed characters. Pip’s determination and intelligence make her a relatable and compelling protagonist, while the story keeps readers guessing until the very end. With its intricate plot and shocking revelations, it’s a page-turner perfect for fans of YA mysteries and crime thrillers.

And Then There Were None

By Agatha Christie

Ten strangers are invited to a secluded island under different pretexts, only to discover they are being accused of past crimes they have committed. One by one, they are mysteriously killed, and with no way to escape, paranoia and suspicion consume them. As the body count rises, the survivors must uncover the killer’s identity before it’s too late, leading to a tense and unforgettable mystery.

4.27

Agatha Christie’s And Then There Were None is a masterclass in suspense and plotting. The novel’s clever structure, mounting tension, and psychological intrigue keep readers guessing until the very end. With its perfectly executed twists and chilling atmosphere, it remains one of the most iconic and compelling mysteries of all time.

The Housemaid

By Freida McFadden

The Housemaid follows a young woman who takes a job as a housemaid in a seemingly perfect home, only to discover that all is not as it seems. As she navigates her new position, she becomes entangled in secrets, lies, and hidden dangers, uncovering the dark undercurrents beneath the household’s polished exterior. Tension and suspense build as she tries to protect herself while unraveling the truth.

4.28

Freida McFadden’s The Housemaid is a gripping psychological thriller full of suspense, twists, and intrigue. The story keeps readers on edge with its fast-paced plot and unexpected revelations, creating a tense and immersive reading experience. With relatable characters and a darkly compelling narrative, it’s a page-turner for fans of domestic thrillers and mysteries.
